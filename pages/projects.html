Visual Navigation and Localization for Autonomous UAVs

IIT Ropar · 2025 – Present

This project focuses on developing robust perception-driven navigation systems that enable UAVs to operate reliably in GPS-denied and dynamic environments. Inspired by biological navigation strategies, the system integrates satellite imagery with real-time onboard perception to achieve resilient localization and adaptive trajectory planning.

The research explores deep visual representations, feature-based matching, and scalable perception pipelines for real-world deployment in applications such as disaster response, search and rescue, and autonomous exploration.

Federated Evolutionary Population-Based Learning for Multi-Robot Systems

IIT Patna · 2025 – Present

Federated Learning (FL) enables collaborative model training without sharing raw data, allowing decentralized agents to learn a global model through local updates and aggregation. Population-Based Training (PBT) optimizes hyperparameters and learning dynamics using asynchronous populations, but current approaches discard underperforming models, limiting recovery and long-term adaptability.

This research proposes integrating Federated Learning, Population-Based Training, and Genetic Algorithms into a unified framework. The system employs island-model based evolutionary strategies, enabling parallel exploration, migration of high-performing solutions, and preservation of diversity across robot populations.

The approach aims to improve stability, scalability, and adaptability in multi-robot environments by combining:

  • Federated aggregation for knowledge sharing
  • Population-based adaptation of hyperparameters
  • Evolutionary island models for parallel optimization

The framework is evaluated on robotic obstacle avoidance and distributed learning tasks, demonstrating improved convergence, robustness, and performance stability in dynamic environments.
